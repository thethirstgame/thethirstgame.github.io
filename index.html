The Thirst: Utes

The early Chernarus survival experience, compressed onto Utes.

The Thirst: Utes is a small-scale survival PvP server inspired by the atmosphere and unrealized promise of early vanilla DayZ.

It compresses that experience onto Utes, where even a very small number of players can create meaningful tension, contested routes, and encounters.

Navigate with the actual map and compass. Read terrain. Plan approaches. Manage food, water, and stamina. Fight with the full character of ArmA 2 mil-sim combat.

The Server

The idea behind The Thirst: Utes is to recapture the dream and atmosphere of early vanilla DayZ while letting players experience the full character of ArmA 2 mil-sim combat.

That means navigating with the actual map and compass, planning routes, reading terrain, choosing approaches, and fighting at distances where bullet drop matters.

It also means actually using the weapons: iron sights, pistol sights, optics, adjustable zeroing, fire-mode switches, and ArmA 2's directional combat audio—the crack of distant gunfire, bullets passing overhead, and the whizz of a near miss.

This is not a server built around hundreds of weapons or constant close-range action. The systems and equipment are deliberately limited so that every resource, route, and gun matters.

Survival

Your character has three central survival statistics:

FOOD WATER STAMINA

Food and water are always slowly draining. The faster you travel, the faster they drain. Walking increases consumption, while running increases it much more heavily.

Stamina is depleted by movement and drains faster as you increase your speed. Travelling on roads is considerably less exhausting than crossing open terrain, hills, or wilderness.

When stamina reaches zero, your character becomes EXHAUSTED.

While exhausted, food and water consumption increases dramatically. Repeatedly pushing your stamina to zero will quickly burn through your water reserves, seriously reducing how far you can travel and how much movement you can afford during combat.

You may still force yourself onward, but exhaustion creates a real survival debt. A long run now can leave you without enough water to complete the next journey, escape a fight, or safely reach another water source.

Rest and allow your stamina to recover fully to 100 to remove exhaustion.

Movement and Exposure

Movement is not only about speed. It also affects how easily other players can locate you.

Running—including crouch-running—while armed with a pistol or rifle temporarily marks your movement, making you easier to spot.

  • Full sprinting is treated as a short combat movement and does not create this marker.
  • Running while unarmed also does not mark you.

This creates a choice between efficient, controlled travel and exposing yourself by moving quickly with a weapon ready.

Progression

Finding an AK-74 is meant to be a major event, not one step in a catalogue of hundreds of guns.

Navigation Equipment

Near military barracks, you may find:

  • watches;
  • compasses.

Maps can appear near camouflaged military buildings and inside towns.

Binoculars can be found at:

  • the northern harbour;
  • the lighthouses;
  • the airstrip control tower.

Navigation equipment matters because players do not begin fully informed and equipped. Learning the island and locating the tools needed to navigate it are part of survival.

Water

Water is found primarily in towns.

The available sources are water pumps and the fountain. Every source contains a finite amount of water, and its supply periodically replenishes.

A player may deliberately consume an entire water source, denying it to anyone who arrives later. This can be used as a tactical survival method—but it also removes that source as a safety net for the player who emptied it.

On Utes, water is not background scenery. It determines routes, creates hotspots, and can be more strategically important than a weapon.
Food

Food is also found in towns.

It appears around chicken coops and rabbit cages. Like water, the available food supply is finite and periodically replenishes.

A town that was useful earlier may already have been stripped by another survivor.

AI Survivors and Bandits

The island currently supports:

  • 6 survivor bots
  • 2 bandits armed with Makarov pistols
  • 1 bandit armed with an AK-74

They periodically respawn and move through the same survival space as human players, creating distant gunshots, temporary danger zones, and armed pressure around important routes.

The AI population can be switched on or off by standing near the flagpole close to the player spawn area.

The bots are not intended to turn Utes into a permanent PvE warzone. They exist to make the island feel inhabited and to create occasional uncertainty while preserving long periods of travel, silence, and tension.

Requirements and Joining

Required: ArmA 2 base game + Operation Arrowhead

Operation Arrowhead alone will not work.

  1. Launch the game without BattlEye.
  2. Open Multiplayer.
  3. Set the server filter to Thirst.
  4. Join The Thirst Utes.
